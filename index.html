Welcome!

I am a fourth year astrophysics Ph.D. student at UC Berkeley (Yes, I share a name with the Carina Nebula). Originally from San Diego, I earned my undergraduate degree from UCLA. I am currently working with Professor Aaron Parsons on the detection of the Epoch of Reionization, a period of history in the early Universe when the first stars and galaxies ionized the neutral hydrogen. To look far back in time (about 13 billion years ago!) and carry out this experiment, we build radio telescopes in South Africa (PAPER and HERA).

In addition to 21cm cosmology, I have research interests in galaxy formation, large-scale structure, optical astronomy, adaptive optics, and black holes. In my free time you can find me traveling, watching movies, teaching, or doing outreach.
